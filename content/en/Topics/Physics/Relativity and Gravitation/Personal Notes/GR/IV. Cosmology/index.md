This chapter turns from local gravitational structure to the universe on the largest scales. It begins with cosmography and observational parametrization, then moves into the standard cosmological model and the broader family of alternative dynamical constructions.

The emphasis here is on keeping kinematics, dynamics, and interpretation together. Cosmology is not only a list of parameters, but a geometric and physical story about large-scale evolution.
