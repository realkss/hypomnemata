Hawking radiation marks the point at which classical black-hole geometry and quantum field theory first become inseparable. The spacetime is still treated classically, but the field theory defined on that background no longer allows a single global notion of vacuum. Once collapse forms a horizon, positive-frequency modes in the asymptotic past do not remain purely positive-frequency in the asymptotic future.

This folder gathers that topic as a small cluster rather than a single isolated page. The central derivation currently present here is the Bogoliubov-transformations route, where the Hawking spectrum emerges from the exponential relation between null coordinates near the horizon.

## Orientation

- classical collapse provides the background geometry
- quantum fields supply the Hilbert space and particle notion
- the thermal factor is fixed by the horizon redshift and surface gravity
- later questions of flux, backreaction, and information begin from this semiclassical result
