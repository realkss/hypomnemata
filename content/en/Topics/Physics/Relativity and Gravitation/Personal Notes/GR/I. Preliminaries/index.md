This chapter gathers the preparation needed before the full dynamical theory appears. It moves from historical orientation through the mathematical language of geometry, then returns through special relativity so that curved spacetime enters as an intelligible extension rather than a sudden abstraction.

The aim here is to keep background material close to later use. Historical notes explain why the subject had to change, mathematical preliminaries provide the language in which it is written, and special relativity fixes the local structure that general relativity later preserves.
