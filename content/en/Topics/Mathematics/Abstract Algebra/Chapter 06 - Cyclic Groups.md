Cyclic groups are generated by a single element, and they are the cleanest groups for learning structure.

## Core definitions

- `G = <g>` means every element of `G` is a power of `g`.
- The **order** of `g` is the least positive `n` with `g^n = e`, if such an `n` exists.

## Key theorems

- Every cyclic group is abelian.
- Every subgroup of a cyclic group is cyclic.
- In a cyclic group of order `n`, `ord(g^k) = n / gcd(n,k)`.
- A cyclic group of order `n` has exactly `phi(n)` generators.

## Counterexamples worth remembering

- `V_4 = Z_2 x Z_2` is abelian but not cyclic.
- Not every nonidentity element is a generator.
  In `Z_6`, only `1` and `5` generate the whole group.

## Companion exercises

1. List all subgroups of `Z_12`.
2. Determine the generators of `Z_18`.
3. Compute the order of `g^4` when `g` has order `20`.
