If $\varphi: G \to H$ is a group homomorphism, the **kernel** is

$\ker(\varphi) = \{g \in G : \varphi(g) = e_H\}$.

The kernel measures how much information the map collapses.

A homomorphism is injective exactly when its kernel is trivial.

## One-line intuition

The kernel is the part of the group that becomes invisible under the homomorphism.
