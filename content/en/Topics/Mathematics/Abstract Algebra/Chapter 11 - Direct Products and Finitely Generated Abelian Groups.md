Direct products build new groups from old ones, and finitely generated abelian groups admit a classification theorem.

## Core definitions

- The **direct product** `G x H` uses componentwise multiplication.
- A finitely generated abelian group is one generated by finitely many commuting elements.

## Key theorems

- `G x H` is a group whenever `G` and `H` are groups.
- In a direct product, order computations reduce to the component orders.
- Every finitely generated abelian group is isomorphic to
  `Z^r x Z_{n1} x ... x Z_{nk}` with `n1 | n2 | ... | nk`.

## Counterexamples worth remembering

- Same order does not imply same abelian structure.
  `Z_8` is not isomorphic to `Z_4 x Z_2`.
- A direct product of cyclic groups need not be cyclic.
  `Z_2 x Z_2` is the standard example.

## Companion exercises

1. Classify the abelian groups of order `8`.
2. Classify the abelian groups of order `18`.
3. Decide when `Z_m x Z_n` is cyclic.
