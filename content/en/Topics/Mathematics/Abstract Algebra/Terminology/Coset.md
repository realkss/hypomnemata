If `H` is a subgroup of `G` and `g` lies in `G`, then

- `gH` is a **left coset**
- `Hg` is a **right coset**

Each coset is a translated copy of the subgroup.

In finite groups, cosets partition the whole group and each coset has the same size as `H`.

## One-line intuition

A coset is what you get by shifting a subgroup through the group.
