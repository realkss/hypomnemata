A **simple group** is a group whose only normal subgroups are the trivial subgroup and the whole group.

Examples:

- $\mathbb{Z}_p$ for prime $p$
- $A_5$ as the classical first nonabelian example

Nonexample:

- $A_4$, which has a normal Klein four subgroup

## One-line intuition

A simple group cannot be broken into a smaller normal quotient structure.
